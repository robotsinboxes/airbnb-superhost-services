What can I help you with?

Transitioning from being an Airbnb traveler to a host was easy for me because I learned what works for guests while staying in Airbnbs in the US, Mexico, Bulgaria, Colombia, Turkey, and more. (It also helps that I have a genuine love and knack for decorating and hosting)!

For many people, the thought of inviting strangers into your house can be nerve-wracking, but I can help alleviate some of those worries by walking you through booking, vetting, security, insurance, payouts, and much more.

As a Superhost, I can also give you best practices for furnishing and designing a unique, memorable guest experience. I can help you market your space with guidance around photography, descriptions, and amenities. Finally, I’ll be available for last-minute questions as you prepare to welcome your first guests.

About me

I’ve traveled all over the world, using Airbnb instead of hotels for the last 10 years. As a way to offset high rent in Brooklyn, I listed my bedroom on Airbnb while I traveled. In 2017, my wife and I took the plunge and purchased a property in New York’s Hudson Valley, with the intention of renting it through Airbnb a couple of weekends a month to subsidize our mortgage.

Just one year later, I was awarded ‘Superhost’ status by Airbnb, as 5 star reviews rolled in and demand for our house continued to increase. In 2021, Airbnb asked me to become a Superhost Ambassador - even featuring me in their ads - so that I could share tips and tricks to help more people learn how to host.
